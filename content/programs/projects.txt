I wasn't initially planning on including anything in these program files beyond the planned course content at first, but they're a nice place to include personal thoughts that aren’t directly in the code. Which is pretty nice, since I tend to be more… wordy? And that type of writing is not exactly suited for subtle clues and hints and stuff. Speaking of writing - the fact that the em-dash is used as an AI indicator is personally devastating to me. I love punctuation marks so much! Unsurprisingly, I've been a long time dasher. The reason I started is because some feedback from my parents when they would proofread for me -in their words- my commas were "almost distractingly overused". Even I have to admit some of those were pretty... rough.

I think it's equally good and unfortunate that many messaging platforms don't include the ability 
to add footnotes. Sometimes I want to include optional content for readers (i.e. my friends) to have
the ability to see any tangential thoughts/ comments I have if they're interested. Especially for things that aren't directly related to the conversation but are topics I'd be equally interested in talking about. However I could easily see myself writing a footnote and wishing I could include a sub footnote to that. A happy medium I've been using (for so long that I don't event think twice about it) is parentheses. 

Anyways, catch ya on the flip side and whatnot. Ideally a lot of the future content on this site will be 
research papers and posters and stuff (fingers crossed, knock on wood, etc.), so the writing displayed 
will be more succinct and pointed. You can think of this as a "behind the scenes" look into the writing process of said papers, which is usually me with stream-of-consciousness word-vomiting onto a page.

So, thanks for reading! And see ya around (or not, you can't really "see" though a website.)
